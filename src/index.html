Featuring 4 Stages With Live Performances By:

Lee Fields

Lee Fields is arguably the greatest soul singer alive today. In an age when the shelf life of an artist largely depends on posturing and trends, he has proven to be an unassailable force of nature. His prolific, decade-spanning career continues to reign supreme on the modern soul scene.

Yung Bae

Yung Bae heralds the rise of Future Funk, stewarding the sub-genre from the underground into a cult-like phenomena. With a sound that spans between funk, smooth jazz, old school hip-hop, and Studio 54 disco – Yung Bae guides us through a parallel universe of funkier times.

Flamingosis

Flamingosis splices together old school soul, wild funk, and D.I.Y. electronic with a radiant message meant for any moment. Collaborating with an all-star cast of musicians on his most recent release, Flamingosis satisfies the sonic palette with Brazilian bounce, tropical house flavor, and chilled beats that redefine funk.

Blu DeTiger (DJ Set)

Bass prodigy and burgeoning pop icon Blu DeTiger took the stage at CBGB when she was only seven. By 17, she was a staple in the city's creative scene. While playing in a multitude of bands, she also distinguished herself as a disco-dishin' DJ who slaps a live bass during her sets.

Bombargo

Bombargo is a Canadian-based band that drops a vibrant splash of soul over their distinctive 'vintage-pop' sound. Well known for bringing unrivaled energy to the stage with sing-along anthems, double-brother harmonies, and a powerful message these boys bring one helluva live show.

Peanut Butter Wolf

Founder of star-spewing independent label Stones Throw Records, Peanut Butter Wolf, is an industry OG. Known for spinning bottomless crates of definitive funk vinyls, raw hip hop beats and genres beyond, PBW's themed sets span decades and genres for a high-energy dance party that will leave your soles smoking.

The Mustache Bash Family Band

A collaboration of funk-lovin' friends comes together once a year to take us for a smooth ride into the best of bygone eras, with classic dance covers sure to have you shakin' your groove thang. This funky bunch always brings that serpentine fire you best believe will bring down the brick house.

The B Sections

A 5-person collective of funk jam enthusiasts assembled from the weekly open jam at Winston's in Ocean Beach – The B Sections seek refuge in the pocket, a place where their high-caliber musical instincts and shared goal of spreading the funky gospel becomes perfectly aligned. Guaranteed to yield funky side-effects.

Discotoshi

This dude might just be the "dilly dilly" of disco. A veteran Basher classically trained in the funkadelic arts, Discotoshi honed his groove hanging with DJs like Rufus Du Sol and Detroit Swindle. He's a cutty crate-digger with a knack for pairing progressive electronic with authentic disco and a healthy dose of live instrumentation.

Abby Normal

Bash godmother Abby Normal has graced the Bash's dancefloor with her all-vinyl, all-genre, all-time turntable set for six years running, and she's back to do it again. This woman drips so much swag, we had to give her an entire stage to contain it. Heed her steeze and be forever funkified.

DJ Dials

Long time DJ and producer, DJ Dials and the current live music scene are one in the same. With an improvisational approach that feeds off crowd energy, his love for good music and great people gives him everything he needs to deliver disco-drenched sets that challenge a crowd to have as much fun as he has doing it.

Starfari

For more than a decade and half, Starfari has been on his musical journey which is settling on a unique blend of popping disco beats infused with psychedelic exurban samples. As a new member of The Bash Family, his innaugural Mustache Bash performance are sure to get things kicking off right on the Fire Stage.

He2She

Mix the funky styles of two OG Bash DJs into one kaleidoscopic concoction, and disco dynamite ignites. This duo of disco dynamos was born at the Bash in 2016, and they've been honing their Bash-focused set over the years to create a performance tailor-made for the funkiest disco party on earth.

Voss & Things

Sparked by a chance encounter on the San Diego dancefloor, VOSS & THINGS are here to make you feel and make you dance. The duo removes the static from the scene – layering live vocals atop eclectic track selections with unparalleled electricity and infectious presence.

Combover

A funky underground phenom hailing from The Bay up north, this disco-endowed music producer draws inspiration from his groovy granddad's record collection. A smooth operator behind the wheels of steel, Combover earthquaking amalgam of funk, disco, house and breaks laces up a vibe so funky, it'll make you wanna slap yo mama!

Venue

The Port Pavilion on Broadway Pier

For an unrivaled arena to showcase of your sexiest '70s self, The Mustache Bash returns to Downtown San Diego's most stunning waterfront venue—the Port Pavilion on Broadway Pier. Suspended above the San Diego Bay, this one-of-a-kind venue features indoor and outdoor staging, a 100-foot bar, food vendors galore and installments from local artists. Bashers will boogie down on the water to a psychedelic light show amid the backdrop of San Diego's glowing cityscape. There's truly no prettier place to party.

Tradition

16 Years of Funkin', 
and It's Only Just Begun

It began with no venue, no stages, no artists or investors; just a group of best buds with a funky vision and a backyard. That vision became a house party where five dollars at the door bought you a bottomless cup, an iPod pumped throwbacks through a makeshift speaker, the living room lit up with disco dance moves, and a family of funky, '70s souls came to fruition. Mustaches were mandatory back then. Dudes and dames alike came clad in show-stopping '70s outfits. And by the time the cops arrived to shut us down, a legendary holiday was born.

16 years later, The Mustache Bash has grown into SoCal's definitive funk-fest, a special day each year when friends get together to set their dancin' spirits asail and fly their funk flags undeniably high.

Roller Disco

Rollin' With the Homies

Just as it was in the '70s, roller-disco was all the craze at last year's Bash. So, we're brining it back for another round! Lace up some complementary skates and come spin your wheels to tunes from underground savants like Combover, Voss & Things, HE2SHE and more - a classy and Bashy lineup guaranteed to keep the fever burnin' hot!

We Are Family

The Mustache Bash Family Band

Like a great mustache, an unforgettable funk band is cultivated through a lifetime of love and a whole lotta soul. Finely groomed into its funkadelic fruition as a perennial favorite at the Mustache Bash, our Family Band is musicianship at its finest, electric at its funkiest, and 'Stache Bash to its core. The all-star squad of local musicians and dancers takes the classics and pedestals them on goldfish platforms with a chemistry only found in that special place and time on the pier. Be sure to stretch before the set—this perennial Basher favorite will have you rump shakin' and cupcakin' for every song!

The Preparty

Get Things Started Off Right

Back in the early days, when the Bash was just a humble house party, the Mustache Bash Family Band was born and bread in a funky little jam den known as Winstons OB. Winstons was the incubator—the iconic live music venue where we watched the Fam Band's legendary members grow into the unstoppable forces they are today. This year, we're headed back to Winstons for the first-ever official Bash Pre Party—a '60s-themed shindig with live jams hosted by Bash veteran, Russell Ramo, and other friends of the Fam Band. We're kickin' it old school for this one, so throw on some tie-dye come down to Winston's OB Friday night for the Mustache Bash warmup celebration!

Frequently Asked Questions

FAQs

Where is The Mustache Bash This Year?

The Bash will be Downtown San Diego at The Port Pavillion on Broadway Pier - 1000 N. Harbor Drive, San Diego, California 92101.

Do I Have to be 21 to Go?

The Mustache Bash is a 21+ event. Make sure to bring your photo ID with proof of age or you will be turned away at the door.

What Time Does the Party Start & End?

The funk starts at 2:00 P.M. and doesn't stop until 11:00 P.M. Get here early, the lineup is stacked and you're not going to want to miss a second!

Can I Bring my Backpack or Purse?

Small handbags, purses, and fanny packs not exceeding 8" x 10" will be permitted, as well as clear tote bags (including handbags and purses) that do not exceed 12" x 6" x 12". Oversized bags (backpacks, suitcases, beach bags, laptop bags, camera bags, etc.) and camelbacks will not be permitted. All persons and bags are subject to search and will be thoroughly inspected upon entry.

Can I Leave and Enter the Bash?

Re-entry will not be permitted at the 2023 Mustache Bash. Fortunately, the venue has plenty of space to roam, and we'll provide everything you'll need for a full day of funk inside. Once you're in, you won't want to leave, anyway.

Should I Roll Solo?

Don't worry if you couldn't convince your crew to join you. You're sure to meet plenty of new friends to replace your fuddy duddy pals.

Will There be Food and Beverage?

There will be food trucks with a variety of quality cuisine available on site to fill your bellies throughout the day and night, and the Stache Bash Bar will be fully stocked all day. Food and beverages will be payable by mobile wallet, card, or cash. Mobile wallet and card are preferred, and fastest.

Can I Volunteer?

We are hiring a small crew of volunteers in exchange for entry. To inquire about volunteering, email us at contact@mustachebash.com.

Can I Smoke or Vape at the Bash?

The Port Pavilion is 100% smoke-free venue, and with that, the Bash is a NON-SMOKING event. No cigarettes, e-cigs, or vapes please.

Can I Bring a Professional Camera?

Cell phone cameras are permitted, and we encourage you to take tons of photos and tag @themustachebash! If they're extra funky, we'll share them on our social channels. Cameras with detachable lenses will not be permitted. We have video and photo teams in place to catch all the magic moments, and we'll be sharing photos in a gallery after the event.

Do I Have to Have a Mustache to Go?

Mustaches are highly encouraged, and you may get some flack if you show up without one. That said, we understand that sometimes circumstances do not permit man fuzz, and won't turn you away. A Basher is family, 'stache or not.

Do Girls Wear Mustaches Too?

Ladies are not expected to grow a mustache. We're funky, not cruel! That said, some ladies do enjoy rocking fake mustaches or finger staches. Grab a sharpie and adorn your finger with the mo' of your liking.

How Dressed Up Should I Get?

It's tradition to go all out! Don't be the only goof without a '70s get-up. A complimentary coatcheck is available, so get weird with your best digs!

How Should I Get There & Back?

You're responsible for getting yourself safely to and from The Bash. Hop on a bus, trolley, or your preferred car service. Please don't drink and drive.

Is the Bash Accessible to People of All Abilities?

Yes, all areas within the Bash site is accessible to people of all abilities. All of the event is on a flat hard surface with accessibility to all areas. If you encounter a staircase at the event, there will also be an elevator option.

What if it Rains?

Rain or Shine the Bash will go on.
